# NOVADISPLAY — European leader in advanced microdisplays for defense, AR, and next-gen consumer optics

## Snapshot

- **Sector:** Semiconductor / Electronic Equipment — advanced microdisplays for near-eye applications
- **HQ:** Western Europe (public research lab spinout, founded late 2000s)
- **Stage:** Growth (lead fund portfolio company since mid-2023)
- **Last round:** €8.5M growth round (late 2025); preceded by €21M round co-led by the lead fund and a sovereign investor (mid-2023)
- **Headcount:** ~81 employees (post consumer carve-out; ~70 FTEs in core microdisplay business)
- **Key investors:** Lead growth fund, sovereign investor, two European VCs, strategic corporate VC (major US equipment vendor), national research fund
- **Internal scores:** DeepTech 0.96 · Patent 331 · Team 8 · Growth 0.10 · Centrality 0.15

## Financial trajectory

The company's revenue history shows a business that scaled rapidly to €23M before a sharp correction driven by the loss of a major geography and production challenges. It has since been restructured around its core defense microdisplay activity — the consumer wearable line was carved out into a separate entity in late 2025. FY25 core microdisplay revenue landed at ~€10.5M (above the €10.3M plan), representing a return to growth after the prior trough. The FY26 budget targets €13–14.3M in revenue (~30% YoY growth), supported by a €15M backlog that exceeds the revenue plan, though Q2 2026 is at risk following a four-month production-line shutdown due to contamination.

## Commercial traction

The core business is selling advanced microdisplays to defense and security OEMs — a tier-1 European defense prime, a leading US sporting optics brand, a premium European optics brand, and other tier-1 programs. The US defense market is the primary growth engine, with a new US headquarters opened in 2025 and a partnership with a major US optics partner announced in mid-2025. FY26 backlog coverage is strong: €15M in firm orders against a €13M revenue budget (115% coverage), indicating the business is now production-limited rather than demand-limited.

The next-generation full-color display technology (internal codename "Aurora") represents a transformational upside. Major-trade-show demonstrations to the three largest US consumer tech platforms generated strong interest, with all three requesting characterization data. Discussions are now shifting toward manufacturability, licensing schemes, and potential acquisition of the technology. The strategic corporate VC invested in late 2024 and is co-funding R&D through a Joint Development Agreement.

## Team & org

The management team was overhauled in early 2025 with the appointment of a new CEO and CFO, replacing the operational co-founder in the day-to-day lead role. A Director of Operations is the key contact for production and yield improvement work. An operating partner (former senior executive at a major US tech company) chairs the board and provides strategic guidance on the US market and large-platform relationships. A second operating partner provides hands-on operational support on manufacturing process improvement.

The consumer carve-out (completed late 2025) transferred the wearable team to a separate entity, leaving the company with ~70 FTEs focused on the core microdisplay business. US commercial expansion is underway with two new hires (Key Account Manager and Technical Sales) and the opening of US headquarters.

## Key risks & watch-items

- **Production fragility / main production line:** A contamination-driven shutdown of the main cluster from early January to late March 2026 (~4 months) severely cut production capacity. While restarted, the safety stock is depleted and Q2 2026 revenue is at risk. Root cause was identified as liner pollution across all chambers — a "zero risk plan" is being implemented but execution must be monitored closely.
- **Gross margin recovery:** FY25 gross margins of ~20% are far below long-term targets (50%+ by FY27). The improvement plan depends on yield gains, new industrial setup, and the elimination of inventory depreciation charges. The operating partner is tracking monthly KPIs, but progress has been slow.
- **Industrial setup / CAPEX overhang:** The current hosted-cleanroom production is capacity-constrained. The preferred plan is to mutualize cleanroom facilities at a nearby former-fab site with a regional industrial group, but this requires political alignment at national agency level and significant CAPEX (€15–33M depending on wafer size). A major equity round (€20M equity + €20M debt) is planned for early 2027.
- **Next-gen technology execution:** Prototype samples have repeatedly encountered quality issues (latest batch proven unusable, no new samples before summer 2026). While large-platform interest is strong, the path from demonstration to licensing or acquisition revenue remains uncertain. A conflict of interest with the strategic corporate VC regarding one of the platforms adds complexity.
- **Shareholder misalignment:** Two of the European VCs are cash-constrained and pushing for a quick technology sale, while the lead fund favors a more stepped, capital-efficient approach protecting the core defense business. The strategic corporate VC is focused exclusively on the next-gen technology. Managing this tension will be critical through the 2027 fundraising cycle.
